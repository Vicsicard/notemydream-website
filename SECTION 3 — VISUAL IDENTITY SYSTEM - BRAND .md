#  **SECTION 3 — VISUAL IDENTITY SYSTEM (Deep-Dive Guide)**

*(This defines how NoteMyDream looks, anywhere it appears.)*

---

# **3.1 — Visual Identity Overview (Your Aesthetic Essence)**

**NoteMyDream’s visual identity feels like:**

✔ **Early morning light**  
 ✔ **Soft, warm, and calming**  
 ✔ **Minimal, uncluttered design**  
 ✔ **Modern but not clinical**  
 ✔ **Reflective and gentle**  
 ✔ **Emotionally safe**

The visual style must evoke the experience of waking up into a soft, quiet moment — before the day becomes noisy.

The design has ONE job:

**Create a quiet, calm visual environment that helps users reflect.**

This is the guiding principle for your entire UI \+ marketing aesthetic.

---

# **3.3 — Typography System (Your “Voice” in Visual Form)**

Typography should match the **brand voice: gentle, modern, minimal.**

### **Primary Typeface:**

**Inter**

Chosen because:

✔ Native-feeling on iOS & Android  
 ✔ Clean, modern, and easy to read  
 ✔ Emotional neutrality  
 ✔ Works for long text (dream transcripts)  
 ✔ Pairs perfectly with soft UI

### **Usage Guidelines**

* **Headings:** Inter SemiBold

* **Subheads:** Inter Medium

* **Body:** Inter Regular

* **Captions / labels:** Inter Regular (90% size)

---

### **Type Hierarchy**

| Use Case | Weight | Size |
| ----- | ----- | ----- |
| H1 (Hero / Screen Title) | SemiBold | 28–32px |
| H2 (Page Section) | SemiBold | 22–24px |
| H3 (In-App Header) | Medium | 18–20px |
| Body Text | Regular | 15–16px |
| Captions | Regular | 12–13px |

**Tone through typography:**  
 Friendly, clear, no decorative fonts, no dramatic italics.

---

# **3.4 — UI Style Principles**

Your UI should always feel:

✔ Spacious  
 ✔ Soft  
 ✔ Gentle  
 ✔ Clear  
 ✔ Easy to breathe in

### **3.4.1 Layout Spacing**

* Base spacing: **8px multiples**

* Use **16–24px padding** in containers

* Maintain **open white space between sections**

### **3.4.2 Cards**

Visual container for reflections, timelines, and prompts:

* Background: `#FFFFFF` or `#E7E6FF`

* Rounded corners: **12–16px**

* Shadow: **very subtle** (0, 1, 3, 10 with opacity \< 0.1)

* Padding: **16–20px**

---

### **3.4.3 Buttons**

#### **Primary Button**

* Background: Dream Blue `#5063FF`

* Text: White

* Shape: Rounded 10–12px OR full-pill

* Example labels:

  * **Record my dream**

  * **Start reflecting**

  * **Continue**

#### **Secondary Button**

* White background

* Dream Blue border

* Dream Blue text

* Used for less-critical actions (skip, maybe later)

---

### **3.4.4 Chat Bubbles (Dream Guide vs User)**

This is core to your app identity.

**User bubble (right):**

* Background: Dream Blue `#5063FF`

* Text: White

* Shape: Rounded with soft corners

* Tone: Calm, personal

**Guide bubble (left):**

* Background: Lavender Mist `#E7E6FF`

* Text: Midnight Indigo

* Tone: Gentle, neutral, supportive

**Spacing:**

* Vertical rhythm: 8–12px

* Horizontal margin: 16–20px

---

### **3.4.5 Backgrounds**

Your default backgrounds must feel like **soft morning light**:

* `#EFF1F5` for main screens

* `#FFFFFF` for cards

* Avoid hard edges or dark UI (unless dark mode added later)

---

# **3.5 — Iconography & Illustration Style**

### **3.5.1 Icons**

Use:

* Simple line icons

* Rounded corners

* Thin or medium strokes

* Minimalistic emotion

Icon categories:  
 Microphone, moon, stars, notebook, chat bubble, timeline, tags.

Avoid:

* Thick, cartoony icons

* Overly colorful icons

* Heavy shadows

---

### **3.5.2 Illustrations (If Used)**

If you use illustrations for onboarding or marketing:

* Soft gradients

* Pastel tones

* Abstract shapes (circles, waves, subtle geometry)

* Gentle morning light aesthetic

Avoid:

* Cartoon characters

* Overly literal dream imagery

* Stock “wellness” graphics

Illustrations should feel like a quiet morning journal moment.

---

# **3.6 — Photography Style (If Using Real Images)**

If you ever incorporate photos (ads, landing page, social):

Choose imagery that feels:

✔ Soft  
 ✔ Warm  
 ✔ Morning  
 ✔ Real  
 ✔ Human

Examples:

* Someone sitting near a window with soft light

* Hand resting on a journal

* Someone waking up slowly

* Calm bedroom scenes

* Soft blankets, warm tones

Avoid:

❌ High-contrast photography  
 ❌ Night-time neon dream themes  
 ❌ Staged stock-photo smiles  
 ❌ Overly spiritual imagery

The brand is about **morning clarity**, not dream mysticism.

---

# **3.7 — Motion & Animation**

Motion must be **gentle, calming, unobtrusive.**

### **Best Practices:**

* Smooth fade-ins

* Soft slide transitions

* Light pulses for recording states

* Ease-out and ease-in-out curves

* 200–350ms timing (calm, unrushed)

### **Avoid:**

❌ Bounce animations  
 ❌ Rapid attention-grabbing motion  
 ❌ Loud transitions

The animation style should match a slow morning stretch.

# **3.10 — Visual Guardrails (What We Must Avoid)**

To maintain consistency and protect the brand:

### **Avoid:**

❌ Harsh neon colors  
 ❌ Dark, moody dream themes  
 ❌ Futuristic or sci-fi aesthetics  
 ❌ Overly mystical imagery (mandalas, dreamcatchers)  
 ❌ Clinical medical blue palettes  
 ❌ Busy layouts  
 ❌ Heavy gradients  
 ❌ Loud, text-heavy screens

Your visual identity must ALWAYS say:

**“Calm, simple, morning clarity.”**
